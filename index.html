Why Tokyo?

Tokyo, endless discovery

Tokyo's history is a tale of transformation and resilience. Originally a modest fishing village named Edo, it rose to prominence during the Tokugawa shogunate, evolving into Japan's de facto capital and a bustling center of trade and culture. Renamed Tokyo during the Meiji Restoration in the late 19th century, the city underwent rapid modernization, embracing Western influences and becoming the heart of Japan's imperial expansion.

However, Tokyo's trajectory was disrupted by the devastation of World War II, particularly the firebombing raids in 1945 that left much of the city in ruins. The city rapidly rebuilt itself, showcasing its resilience and determination to thrive. Today, Tokyo stands as a global metropolis, renowned for its innovation, cultural richness, and vibrant energy that characterizes contemporary Japan.

The restaurant has an atmosphere where art-nouveau and Japanese style meet, and it is just like a museum. All private rooms are equipped with hot griddles, where your dedicated chef cooks in front of you. You can overlook Omotesando Street from the fully encased glass counter seats.
